Reasoning for Large Language Models (rStar)

Year: 2024 With: Christine Schütt, Emil Sjöberg, Sara Sterlie Dataset: GSM8K

We tested how robust the rStar reasoning method is when things aren't ideal — irrelevant noise in the question, confounding examples that break stereotypes, and different generation settings — to see whether its reasoning gains actually hold up.

rStar improves reasoning in small language models without any fine-tuning, using a generator and discriminator model working together through Monte Carlo Tree Search. We compared four variations on GSM8K: the regular method, a version with injected noise, one tested on counter-stereotypical gender examples, and one with adaptive temperature during token generation. The discriminator added no measurable benefit, injected noise only hurt performance slightly, and rStar made the same mistakes as GPT-4 on counter-stereotypical examples — but adaptive temperature gave a clear accuracy boost.
